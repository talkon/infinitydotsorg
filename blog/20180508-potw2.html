POTWs 2

8 May 2018

Every week, we will select three notable or interesting problems, marked with E,M,H (“easy”, “medium”, “hard”) for the relative difficulty. Easy problems will be around TMO or easier than TMO; medium problems will be around Oct Camp / easy IMO, and hard problems will be around medium / hard IMO.

E2 [@Konigsberg on AoPS] In a convex pentagon, show that we can choose $3$ diagonals such that their lengths can form a triangle.

M2 [China TST 2007 Quiz] Let $I$ be the incenter of triangle $ABC.$ Let $M,N$ be the midpoints of $AB,AC,$ respectively. Points $D,E$ lie on $AB,AC$ respectively such that $BD=CE=BC.$ The line perpendicular to $IM$ through $D$ intersects the line perpendicular to $IN$ through $E$ at $P.$ Prove that $AP\perp BC.$

H2 [Google CodeJam 2011] Goro wants to sort a list of $n$ distinct numbers in an increasing order. In each round, Goro can fix some elements of the list. All non-fixed elements of the list will then be permuted randomly (with each permutation having equal probability.) Given $n$ and the initial list, determine the expected number of rounds Goro will need to sort the list, under Goro's best strategy.
